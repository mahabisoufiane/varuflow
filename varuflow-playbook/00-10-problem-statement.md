# Stage 0–10: Problem Statement

The goal of this stage is to write down — in one sentence — who you serve, what pain you remove, and what they pay for. Everything built later must trace back to this.

---

## The One-Sentence Statement

> **Varuflow helps Swedish small and mid-size businesses that use Fortnox manage their inventory and operations from a single SaaS dashboard, replacing spreadsheets and manual ERP work.**

---

## The Three Pillars

### 1. The User

Small business owners and operations managers in Sweden who:

- Already use Fortnox for accounting/invoicing
- Manage physical inventory (products, stock levels, warehouses)
- Are frustrated by toggling between spreadsheets, Fortnox, and email
- Have 1–50 employees, no dedicated IT team

### 2. The Painful Job

Before Varuflow, this user:

- Manually updates stock counts in spreadsheets
- Cross-references Fortnox invoices to check what was sold vs. what is in stock
- Has no real-time view of low-stock alerts or reorder triggers
- Wastes hours per week reconciling inventory with accounting data

### 3. The Outcome They Pay For

> "I can see my exact stock levels, get alerted before I run out, and my Fortnox invoices stay in sync automatically — without touching a spreadsheet."

This is the outcome. Every feature either contributes to this or goes to the backlog.

---

## Validation Questions

Before moving to Stage 10–30, answer these honestly:

- [ ] Can you name 3 real people who have this exact problem today?
- [ ] Have any of them ever paid for a solution (spreadsheet consultant, custom tool, etc.)?
- [ ] Is the pain frequent enough that they'd think about it at least weekly?
- [ ] Is Fortnox integration the key differentiator, or could a generic inventory tool solve it?

---

## What Varuflow Is NOT (Scope Guard)

| Out of scope for MVP | Why |
|----------------------|-----|
| Full ERP replacement | Too broad, too slow to build |
| Multi-currency / multi-country tax | Add after first 10 paying customers |
| AI demand forecasting | Nice-to-have, not core value |
| POS / point-of-sale | Different user, different job |
| HR or payroll | Unrelated to inventory outcome |
